About Us

The First Settlement Derby Dames (FSDD) is a roller derby league that is committed to providing an opportunity for all women to strengthen and challenge themselves through the sisterhood of roller derby. They support their community through volunteering and participating in fund-raisers for local charities.

Hades Ladies

2014 Hades Ladies Who Are the Derby Dames? FSDD is comprised of moms, sisters, and the girl-next-door. They are local business owners, professionals, college students, and the stay at home moms who have come together to play a sport they love (usually by the end of the first practice).

About Derby


What's This Derby Stuff?


Leo A. Seltzer had a vision in 1935: an endurance race involving 25 teams roller skating on a banked track. Defensive and offensive skaters soon came into play as the competition evolved from an endurance race to a game emphasizing mainly on skaters' falls and collisions with one another, elbowing, and "whipping" and slamming each other into the track's outer rail. The transformation of the race became the game of roller derby.

How is Roller Derby Played?


The game is actually called a bout. The bout is a series of jams that can last up to two minutes, with 30 seconds between each jam; there are two 30 minute periods with a break between each period.
Each team has five players. The jammer they have the stars on their helmet; they score the points. The pivot they have the stripe on their helmet; they control the pack. The blockers they do not have any identifiers on their helmets; they help the jammer get through the pack.
The referee blows the whistle; the pack starts to skate; the referee blows the whistle again, then the jammers start to skate. The blockers try to block the opposing team's jammer from making their way through the pack, while propelling their own jammer forward through the pack. On the initial lap, the first jammer to pass all of the opposing team becomes lead jammer. The lead jammer can call off the jam. After the initial lap the jammers scores one point for each opposing team's players they pass. When the lead jammer calls off the jam or the two minute period ends, the jam stops and the officials calculate the score.
